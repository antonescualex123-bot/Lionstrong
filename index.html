What 69.8% Auto Meant

With nearly 70% in auto, the account had no keyword-level control. Amazon's algorithm decided where money went. No bid strategy, no placement control, no defense of high-value search terms. Revenue happened by chance, not by design.

The Harvesting Process

Auto and broad campaigns continuously discover converting search terms. Once a term proves performance, it gets harvested into an Exact Match campaign — giving full control over bid, placement, and budget. Auto spend on that term is then reduced, making room for the next wave of discovery.

Why Exact ACoS Looks Higher

Exact campaigns run with aggressive bids intentionally — the goal is to win auctions consistently on proven, high-intent keywords. This drives sales velocity, which Amazon treats as an organic ranking signal. Part of the exact match investment pays for ranking, not just for the immediate sale. Competition on these terms is also significantly more intense than in auto auctions.

Wasted Spend — Why the Definition Matters

The same account produces very different "waste" figures depending on the threshold used

The 1-Click Problem

Any active Amazon account generates thousands of search terms with exactly one click and zero orders. This is structurally unavoidable — it is how discovery works. Counting these as "wasted spend" produces a figure of ~25% on this account. That number cannot be acted upon: you cannot negate terms at 1 click without also negating the next best-performing keyword before it has a chance to convert. The figure is technically accurate and completely meaningless as a management signal.

10+ Clicks: The Actionable Threshold

At 10+ clicks with zero orders, a term has had statistically sufficient exposure to demonstrate non-performance. These are the only terms worth negating. On this account, that figure is 1.34% — down from 3.58% in the same period last year, on a budget that doubled.

Where This Goes From Here

The account is in a healthy scaling phase. Discovery is feeding a growing exact match portfolio, efficiency metrics are improving year-over-year on a doubled budget, and actionable waste is at 1.34%. The structure is working as designed.

The next phase of optimization focuses on progressively reducing auto spend on already-harvested terms — redirecting that budget toward new discovery or scaling proven exact match campaigns further. As the exact portfolio matures, overall ACoS will compress without sacrificing volume.

High-ACoS terms (>50%, orders present) represent 0.39% of total spend and are continuously addressed through automated bid optimization. No manual intervention is required at current levels — automated systems handle this as part of standard account management.
