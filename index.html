How Wasted Spend is Calculated — and Why It Matters

The threshold used to define "wasted spend" has a dramatic impact on the resulting figure

The 1-Click Problem

A Search Term Report for any active Amazon account will contain thousands of search terms that received exactly one click and zero orders. This is completely normal — a single click provides no statistically meaningful signal about whether a term converts or not. Counting these as "wasted spend" produces an alarming number that has no actionable basis. On this account, applying a 1-click threshold produces a waste figure of ~25% — a number that sounds critical but cannot be acted upon in any meaningful way.

The Right Approach: 10+ Click Threshold

A search term only becomes a candidate for negation or bid reduction once it has accumulated enough clicks to draw a statistically valid conclusion. The industry-standard minimum is 10 clicks with zero orders. At this threshold, the actionable waste on this account is 1.34% — a figure that is both honest and manageable.

Why You Cannot Eliminate the "Noise"

Discovery traffic works as a funnel. For every term that eventually converts at click 12, there are dozens of terms that get 1 click and never return. These single-click impressions are the cost of finding profitable new keywords. Aggressively negating at 1-2 clicks would cut this discovery mechanism — reducing the noise figure on paper while eliminating the engine that generates €155,393 in sales at 11.3% ACoS.

If You Negate at 1 Click

Waste % drops to near zero on paper. But you also negate thousands of terms before they have a chance to convert — including the ones that would have become your best performers. Net effect: lower sales, higher overall ACoS.

If You Negate at 10+ Clicks

Only terms with statistically proven non-performance are removed. Discovery continues uninterrupted. The account finds new converting keywords continuously while eliminating only confirmed waste.

The Result on This Account

1.34% actionable waste. 44.8% of sales from discovery traffic at 16.0% ACoS. This is what a well-managed discovery funnel looks like in practice.

Discovery → Harvesting → Exact Match
How discovery spend converts into increasingly efficient exact match campaigns over time

The Harvesting Flywheel

Auto and broad campaigns act as a continuous keyword research engine. When a search term accumulates enough converting data, it gets harvested into a dedicated Exact Match campaign where bids can be precisely controlled. Over time, the Exact portfolio grows with proven performers while spend on discovery is reduced for those specific terms — improving efficiency without sacrificing volume. This is an ongoing, compounding process: the longer it runs, the more refined the account becomes.

What 69.8% Auto Means

When nearly 70% of spend runs through auto with low bids, the account is entirely dependent on Amazon's algorithm to decide where money goes. There is no keyword-level control, no placement strategy, and no ability to defend specific search terms against competitors. Revenue happens by chance, not by design.

What the Shift to Exact Means

Every percentage point moved from auto to exact represents a keyword that has been proven, harvested, and brought under full control. Bid, placement modifier, dayparting, budget — all managed at the keyword level. The account stops relying on Amazon's decisions and starts making its own.

Organic Ranking as a Side Effect

Consistent ad spend on an exact match keyword drives consistent sales velocity on that keyword. Amazon's ranking algorithm treats sales velocity as a relevance signal — meaning exact match campaigns actively improve organic position over time. This is why exact ACoS appears higher: part of the investment is paying for ranking, not just for the immediate sale.

Discovery (auto + broad) runs at 16.0–16.6% ACoS using intentionally low bids — the goal is to find converting terms at minimal cost, not to dominate auctions. Exact match operates differently: bids are set aggressively to win auctions consistently on proven, high-intent keywords. This serves two purposes beyond immediate sales — cleaner traffic quality (exact queries have stronger purchase intent) and organic ranking improvement (consistent sales velocity on a keyword signals relevance to Amazon's algorithm). The higher ACoS on exact (20.8%) is not inefficiency — it is the deliberate cost of owning those keywords. Competition is significantly more aggressive on exact auctions precisely because these are the terms every competitor also wants. The current split — 75% discovery, 25% exact — is healthy for an account actively scaling its keyword portfolio.

Summary

How Wasted Spend Figures Can Be Inflated

Using a 1-click threshold produces a waste figure of ~25% on this account — a number that sounds alarming but is methodologically unsound. At 1 click, there is no statistical basis to classify a term as wasted. Any analysis using this threshold is designed to create urgency, not to reflect reality.

The Same Traffic Generates 44.8% of All Sales

The discovery mechanism that produces single-click, zero-order terms is the same mechanism that generated €155,393 in sales at 11.3% ACoS in the last 63 days. Eliminating "noise" at 1 click means eliminating the engine behind nearly half of all revenue.

Actual Actionable Waste: 1.34%

At the correct 10+ click threshold, actionable wasted spend is 1.34% of total spend in 2026 — down from 3.58% in the same period last year. Achieved while total spend more than doubled (+109%).

High-ACoS Spend Down 83% as a Rate

Terms with orders but ACoS >50% dropped from 2.31% to 0.39% of total spend — from €658 to €233, on a budget that doubled. Continuously monitored and addressed through automated bid optimization.
